On the Subject of The Colored Peg

It is BBQ, doesn't it?

The module consists of two buttons "YES" and "no", a peg with 5 colored faces, 3 colored LEDs, and indicator current-passing stages. The colors on the module can be: red, green, blue, white, black, yellow, cyan and magenta. Pass 3 stages in order to solve the module. When you press the button at the wrong time or press the wrong button, the module will handle a strike. The first face of the peg has a small circle on it. Note: White is RGB, Yellow is RG, Cyan is GB, Magenta is RB, Black is nothing.

Stage 1

On the first stage, press "YES", if more than 4 conditions apply.

  • The peg has exactly 3 colors (Example: MMGGR).
  • Every color of LEDs present on the peg.
  • Amount of faces has red in it is more than 2.
  • Bomb has lit FRQ.
  • All colors on the peg are different.
  • On the peg and on the LEDs are exactly 2 blue color.
  • Bomb has a module “Colour Flash”.
  • Bomb has less than 4 batteries.
  • Bomb has DVI port.
  • The last digit of serial number is even.
  • The peg has a black and a white color.

On the first stage, you can press the button at any time.

Stage 2

Count red, green and blue on LEDs and on the peg in both stages (Example: if LEDs are RMG count will be R2 G1 B1). Let's name this number as X, Y, Z. The top left coordinate is (0, 0). Table is wrapped around it. X is a row, Y is a column, and Z is a multiplier. Find the intersection of a row and a column. Multiply it by a multiplier. Count such from the top left angle. This is your resulting number. If this number is more than 6, press "YES", otherwise press "No".

Press button, when last digit of timer is equal to amount red(including blending) on both stages, modulo 9.

Stage 3

Convert all colors into digit and logical operations using table below. Find two values:
Value 1: "(A [1] (B [2] С)) [4] C"
Value 2: "B [3] (C [4] (A [5] C))"
[n] is operation n color on the peg in order appearing on the module. A, B, C is values LEDs.

Color Operation Value
Red OR 10110
Green AND 11111
Blue XOR 00010
Magenta IMPLICATION 10001
Yellow NAND 10111
Cyan NOR 11010
White XNOR 11100
Black AND 01101

If the second value is greater than the first, press "NO", otherwise press "YES". You should press the button when the smallest of two values modulo 10 is the last digit of the bomb's timer.
